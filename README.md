**My launch philosophy:**

I don't believe in launching behind paywalls or in isolation. I build in public, share progress, and let the community validate before I optimize. When a product is ready:

1. **Reddit** — Write a genuine post showing what I built and why. No hype, just the product. The right subreddit finds the right buyer.
2. **Discord servers** — Drop into technical communities, show demos, answer questions. Real engagement converts better than any ad.
3. **Slack workspaces** — Professional buyers live here. Higher intent, higher price tolerance.
4. **Free launches first** — Give something for free, collect feedback, then price the full version. Builds trust faster than anything.

**Why this works:** Technical communities on Reddit and Discord have some of the highest-intent buyers on the internet. They know exactly what they need, they evaluate products rigorously, and they tell their networks when something is good.
